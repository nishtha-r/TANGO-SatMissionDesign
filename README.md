<h1> 🛰️ TANGO-Sat Mission Design </h1> 
Senior Capstone Design Project — University of Florida, Department of Mechanical & Aerospace Engineering

<h2>Description</h2>

TANGO-Sat is a conceptual 6U CubeSat mission designed to demonstrate autonomous rendezvous and proximity operations in Low Earth Orbit (LEO). As Astrodynamics Lead, I owned the mission's orbital design end-to-end: trajectory analysis, launch vehicle selection, maneuver planning, ΔV budgeting, and end-of-life disposal, taking the mission from concept through a validated STK Astrogator simulation.

<h3>My role covered</h3>  
- Mission orbit and trajectory design <br/>
- Launch vehicle trade study (weighted decision matrix) <br/>
- Orbital mechanics calculations and ΔV budgeting <br/>
- STK Astrogator mission simulation and maneuver planning <br/>
- Orbital insertion and transfer maneuver design <br/>
- End-of-life deorbit strategy compliant with orbital debris mitigation guidelines <br/>

<h3>Launch Vehicle Trade Study</h3>  

Candidate launch providers were scored using a weighted decision matrix against:

- Launch reliability
- Cost
- Ride-share compatibility
- Payload capacity
- Mission requirements

**Result:** The Falcon 9 Block 5 was selected as the optimal launch vehicle.
